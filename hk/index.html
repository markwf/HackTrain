THE HACKTRAIN IS COMING TO HONG KONG!


GET READY FOR THE BIGGEST HACKATHON OF THE YEAR!


HACKTRAIN:
/'HAK'TREÉªN/
Noun.



The HackTrain is arguably one of the craziest and most memorable hackathon experiences on earth! We've organised 3 hackathons in the UK, but this November The HackTrain is coming to Hong Kong to bring you HackTrain HK, a weekend-long hacking adventure on Hong Kong's mass transit railway system! 40 of the very best developers, designers and entrepreneurs will be building apps, websites and hardware that tackles the industry's biggest challenges!


48 HOURS. 8 VENUES. 40 PARTICIPANTS. #HACKTRAINHK

Schedule




Friday

Evening

MTR Headquarters, Kowloon Bay

Hackers have the opportunity to meet senior MTR stakeholders, learn about datasets available for the event and also receive more detail about challenges/theme of the competition.

Friday

Night

Marriot Courtyard Sha Tin

Participants pitch their ideas to each other. Best 10 ideas are carried forward to be developed during the hackathon. Team's sleep overnight at the Marriot Hotel Marriot Courtyard Sha Tin in rooms.

Saturday

Morning

Tsim Sha Tsui Station

Experience first hand some of the challenges that MTR staff and passengers face when using the network!

Saturday

Afternoon

Blueprint

Begin hacking at one of Hong Kong's top coworking spaces! We will be joined by some amazing mentors from HK's rail + tech community.

Saturday

Evening

Travel to Lantau Island

We'll be taking the MTR and cable car (Ngong Ping 360) to one of HK's most famous highlands, Ngong Ping.

Saturday

Night

Ngong Ping Village

Work overnight at the top of Ngong Ping Village, near the Po Lin Monastery! Participants will be able to sleep over in the venue.

Sunday

Morning

Siu Ho Wan Train Depot

Continue hacking at one of MTR's largest train depots! Participants will be able to see trains being worked on by MTR engineers whilst they work on their prototypes.

Sunday

Afternoon

MTR OCC Viewing Gallery

Participants will present their prototypes at MTR's Operations Control Centre. This is where MTR keep a close watch on network activity to ensure the delivery of safe and reliable services each day.

Challenges


The hackathons will provide challenges and datasets to the participants of the hackathon.
This gives participants the opportunity to work on projects that can have an impact on the whole industry.



Customer Experience

  • Passenger Information during Incident
  • Ticketing and Capacity Management (NP360)
  • Journey Entertainment
  • Applications of New Technologies

Operations Efficiency

  • Crowd Management
  • Train Capacity Utilisation
  • In-station Movement
  • Safety Enhancement
